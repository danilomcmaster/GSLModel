\section{Introduction}
The Great Salt Lake is of great ecological importance to Utah's environment. In recent years, its footprint has been shrinking. Understanding the dynamics of the lake, specifically the rate at which it is shrinking and what it would take to reverse that shrinking, will allow researchers and citizens of Utah to understand how much time the Great Salt Lake has left. In this paper, we design and analyze a differential equation to understand the dynamics of the Great Salt Lake. The differential equation is comprised of an inflow function and an outflow function and we consider how changes in the inflow and outflow of the Lake will affect its overall volume.
